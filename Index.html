Kimi Räikkönen

When driving Formula 1 is just your hobby
  1. Started his journey in F1 in 2001 with Sauber with only 23 races to his name
  2. Cited as inexperienced, there were concerns with him getting his superlicence at such a young age
  3. He was reportedly asleep 30 minutes before his debut, in the end managed to score points
  4. Landed a seat at McLaren after an impressive first year in F1
  5. In 2003 he achieved 2nd place in the WDC, losing only by 2 points to Michael Schumacher
  6. Legendary Kimi moment: after a decent start to the 2006 Monaco GP, the Finn suffered a mechanical failure and had to retire the race; Straight after jumping our from the car, he was seen walking over to his yacht and having drinks with his friends
  7. In 2007 he signed a multi-year contract with Ferrari alongside Felipe Massa
  8. In the same year he became a World Champion
  9. He stayed with them for 3 years, after which he decided he wanted to try a more Finnish sport: Rallying
  10. In the end he found his way back to F1, signing up with Lotus in 2012
  11. Eventually he was picked up once again by Ferrari, where he stayed from 2014 to 2018
  12. His last win in F1 came in the 2018 US GP
  13. In 2019 the story made a full circle, as he joined Alfa Romeo, which was a team originating from Sauber
  14. During one of pre-season interviews he stated his rather unconventional approach to being an F1 driver: It’s more like a hobby for me. So, obviously, I don’t need to do it if I don’t want.
  15. At the 2020 Eifel Grand Prix, he broke the record for most starts in Formula One, making him one of the most experienced drivers in the sport
  16. Known as the "The Iceman", Kimi was never to keen on giving interviews and participating in various media events. However that is the opposite for his interaction with fans as can be seen on this video
  17. He is also known for his iconic radio quotes
  18. In 2021 he announced his retirement from F1, putting an end to one of the most iconic careers in the sport

    19. This is it. This will be my last season in Formula 1. This is a decision I did during last winter. It was not an easy decision but after this season it is time for new things. Even though the season is still on, I want to thank my family, all my teams, everyone involved in my racing career and especially all of you great fans that have been rooting for me all this time. Formula 1 might come to an end for me but there is a lot more in life that I want to experience and enjoy. See you around after all of this!

    Sincerely Kimi
